03

The big concepts

You know all of these from ZBrush land, but they cost very different amounts on a phone. For each one, tell me how central it is to how you personally sculpt. That tells me where the performance budget goes.

Dynamic topology

The mesh adds triangles only where you sculpt, so detail appears where you need it and the rest stays light. Sculptris and Nomad style. The alternative is pre-densifying everything, which phones hate.

HOW CENTRAL IS THIS TO YOU?

Global remesh (the Dynamesh move)

One tap rewraps the whole model with fresh even topology so you can keep pulling clay without stretched polygons. The reset-and-keep-going button.

HOW CENTRAL IS THIS TO YOU?

Subdivision levels

A ladder of resolutions on the same clean mesh. Drop to level 1 to move big forms, ride up to level 6 for pores, and detail survives the trip. The disciplined alternative to Dynamesh-everything.

HOW CENTRAL IS THIS TO YOU?

Masking

Paint a protective layer so brushes only affect what's exposed. Invert it, blur it, sharpen it. In ZBrush this is half the workflow. On a phone the question is how much of that half you actually need.

HOW CENTRAL IS THIS TO YOU?

Multi-object scenes

Separate meshes living in one scene, like subtools. Eyes stay spheres, teeth stay separate, you sculpt one thing at a time and export together or apart.

HOW CENTRAL IS THIS TO YOU?

Matcaps

Baked material-and-lighting spheres that make the model read as clay, metal or wax instantly, no light setup. Cheap on the GPU, huge for judging forms.

HOW CENTRAL IS THIS TO YOU?

Decimation for export

Crunch the triangle count way down while keeping the shape, so the file that leaves the phone is light enough to email and clean enough to remesh in ZBrush.

HOW CENTRAL IS THIS TO YOU?

Transform gizmo

Arrow and ring handles for precise move, rotate and scale, ZBrush gizmo or Rhino style, instead of nudging things around with a grab brush.

HOW CENTRAL IS THIS TO YOU?
