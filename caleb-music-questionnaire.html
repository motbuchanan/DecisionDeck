The big pieces

A full music app is built from a handful of core ideas. For each one, tell me how much it's part of how you work. If one is new to you, that's genuinely useful, it tells me whether to build it, teach it, or skip it.

Loop vs full arrangement

Some people live in an 8-bar loop forever. Some build the whole song with an intro, verses, a hook and an ending. A full app has to do both, but knowing where you spend your time decides what the app opens into.

HOW MUCH IS THIS YOUR WORLD?

Programmed parts + recorded audio together

The big one for a real app. Built parts (drums, bass, keys you tap or play in) and recorded audio (your voice, a guitar, a file off the Zoom) living on the same timeline, in time with each other. This is the line between a beat pad and a studio.

HOW MUCH IS THIS YOUR WORLD?

The timeline

A left-to-right view of the whole song where every track sits in its lane and you see how it all lines up. Scroll through time, grab a chunk, move it. The workspace a finished song gets built on.

HOW MUCH IS THIS YOUR WORLD?

Recording into the app

Hit record and lay your voice or an instrument down over what's already playing, right there in the app. Do a take, do another, keep the one you like. The whole reason to have a mic in your hand.

HOW MUCH IS THIS YOUR WORLD?

Sampling and chopping

Grab any sound (a record, a voice memo, something you recorded) and cut it into pieces spread across pads, then play the pieces in a new order to make something new. The heart of a lot of hip-hop and lo-fi.

HOW MUCH IS THIS YOUR WORLD?

Mixing and effects

Every track gets its own volume, its own place in the stereo field, and a chain of effects: EQ to carve space, compression for punch, reverb and delay for depth. The difference between a rough idea and something that sounds finished.

HOW MUCH IS THIS YOUR WORLD?

Feel: swing, timing, humanness

Whether the app locks everything to a rigid grid or lets things breathe. Swing bounces the groove. Playing loose and keeping it loose keeps the human feel. For someone who works by feel, this matters more than most features.

HOW MUCH IS THIS YOUR WORLD?

Phone to computer handoff

You edit on the phone and the computer. So the question is how finished the app has to get things on its own, versus how cleanly it hands a project (or separate track files) off to whatever you use on the desktop to finish. This decides how deep the app goes.
